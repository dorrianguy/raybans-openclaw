## 💰 Tier 1: High Revenue, Clear Market (Build First)

### 1. Inventory Vision — Retail Inventory Automation
- **Full spec:** `INVENTORY-VISION-SPEC.md`
- **Target:** Retail store owners, warehouse managers, inventory service companies
- **Pain point:** Physical inventory counts take days and cost $3-10K per count
- **How:** Walk aisles, glasses snap shelves, AI identifies products + counts via barcode/visual recognition
- **Pricing:** $79/mo (solo store), $199/mo (multi-store), $499/mo (enterprise)
- **Market:** $2-3B US retail inventory services market
- **Revenue potential:** $80-150K/mo at 1,000 stores
- **Competitive edge:** 10x cheaper than RGIS ($3-10K per count), 10x cheaper hardware than Zebra ($2K+ per device)

### 2. Inspection-as-a-Service — Professional Visual Documentation
- **Target:** Home inspectors, property managers, insurance adjusters, construction foremen, building code inspectors
- **Pain point:** Inspectors spend 50% of their time on documentation — writing notes, taking photos, organizing reports
- **How:** Walk through property, glasses auto-document everything, AI generates professional inspection report with photos + annotations + findings
- **Pricing:** $149/mo (individual inspector), $399/mo (inspection company), $999/mo (enterprise / insurance)
- **Market:** 4.3M home inspections/year in US alone. Insurance inspections are even bigger.
- **Revenue potential:** Even 2,000 inspectors at $149/mo = $298K/mo
- **The killer angle:** Inspection reports are liability documents. AI-generated with photo evidence is MORE reliable than handwritten notes.

### 3. Field Service AI — Hands-Free Technical Assistance
- **Target:** HVAC technicians, electricians, plumbers, auto mechanics, IT field techs
- **Pain point:** Technicians stop working to look up manuals, wiring diagrams, part numbers, error codes. Every pause costs $150-300/hr in billable time.
- **How:** Look at the unit/system, glasses identify model, AI pulls up relevant manual pages, wiring diagrams, common issues, and talks the tech through the repair
- **Pricing:** $99/mo per tech, $499/mo per company (10 techs), enterprise custom
- **Market:** 6M+ field service workers in the US. Field service software market is $5.2B.
- **Revenue potential:** Massive. Even niche play in HVAC alone is a $50M+ opportunity.
- **Additional revenue:** Parts identification → referral fees from parts suppliers

## 💰 Tier 4: Platform Plays (Long-term, Massive Upside)

### 13. Vision App Store / Marketplace
- **The play:** Build the platform, let others build vision apps on top
- **How:** SDK + marketplace. Third-party developers create specialized vision agents. We take 20-30% of revenue.
- **Why:** We can't build for every industry. Let the experts build for their own domains.
- **Revenue:** Platform fees + developer subscriptions + transaction cuts
- **This is the App Store model** applied to smart glasses AI.

### 14. Enterprise Vision Platform (Vigil Integration)
- **The play:** Vigil + smart glasses = enterprise AI with eyes
- **How:** All the features above, but wrapped in Vigil's enterprise security/governance layer. RBAC, audit trails, DLP for visual data, compliance.
- **Pricing:** Enterprise contracts: $50K-500K/year
- **Target:** Fortune 500 companies that need governed AI vision across their workforce
- **The Vigil play:** Smart glasses become another data source that Vigil governs

### 15. Data Licensing (Aggregated, Anonymized)
- **The play:** Aggregated visual data about retail shelves, product placement, pricing
- **How:** With user consent, aggregate anonymized shelf data across thousands of stores
- **Who pays:** CPG companies (Procter & Gamble, Unilever) pay MILLIONS for shelf placement data. IRI and Nielsen charge $100K+/year for this data.
- **Revenue:** Could be the biggest revenue stream if the user base gets large enough
- **Critical:** Must be opt-in, anonymized, GDPR/CCPA compliant

- **Pain point:** Crime scene documentation is the foundation of criminal prosecution — 40% of cold cases cite "inadequate documentation" as a factor. CSIs spend 2-6 hours photographing scenes, placing evidence markers, and hand-drawing sketches. Photos must follow strict protocols (overall → medium → close-up, scale references, multiple angles). Missing a single piece of evidence or angle can make evidence inadmissible. Evidence contamination from repeated entries to photograph missed items is a major problem.
- **How:** CSI wears glasses while processing the scene. AI auto-catalogs every evidence item: type classification (biological, trace, digital, physical), sequential numbering, GPS + relative position mapping, scale estimation from known reference objects (evidence markers, door frames). Photo protocol assistant: "Evidence Item 7 — close-up captured. Need 45-degree angle shot and overall context shot." Auto-generates chain-of-custody photo log with timestamps, photographer ID, and metadata. 3D-ish scene reconstruction from walk-through: relative positions of all evidence items. Voice notes attached to specific evidence items: "Item 12: Red fiber on door handle, possible transfer from suspect clothing."
- **Pricing:** $199/mo per investigator, $999/mo per crime lab (10 investigators + case management), $4,999/mo per department (unlimited + evidence management integration + court presentation tools). Federal agency tier: custom pricing.
- **Why 10x:** A CSI earns $55-85K/year and spends 40% of their time on documentation. Reducing documentation time by 50% = one additional case per week per investigator. A botched evidence photo that loses a conviction costs the justice system $100K+ per case. Proper documentation reduces successful evidence challenges by 60%.
- **Market size:** Forensic science services: $18B globally. Crime scene investigation: $5B. 16,000+ law enforcement agencies in the US. 13,000+ crime labs. Digital forensics: $7B. Evidence management software: $1.5B. DOJ technology grants: $3B+/year.

The LCES safety protocol (Lookout, Communication, Escape routes, Safety zones) requires constant situational awareness that's impossible to maintain while also fighting fire. Fire behavior analysts observe from lookout points using binoculars and radio observations — they can only monitor one part of a fire at a time. Post-fire mapping (burned area reflectance classification) requires satellite or aerial surveys days later. 2025 was the worst US fire season ever — 15M acres burned, $50B+ in damages.
- **How:** Firefighter wears glasses on the line. AI monitors: flame length estimation (critical for crew safety — flames >4ft = too dangerous for direct attack), wind speed/direction from vegetation movement and smoke patterns, spot fire detection (embers landing ahead of the main fire = escape route cut off), terrain slope analysis (fire moves 4x faster for every 20% increase in slope). Safety alerts: "WARNING: Wind shift detected. Fire spreading upslope toward your position. Escape route to access road in 800m, bearing 270." Prescribed burn monitoring: "Head fire approaching containment line. Flame length 2.5ft. Spotting distance 15ft. Holding." Auto-mapping: GPS track of the fire perimeter as crews walk the edge. Post-fire: burned area severity mapping from walk-through.
